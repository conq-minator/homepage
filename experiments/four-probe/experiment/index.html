Theory

Principle and Formulae

The four probe method separates current carrying and voltage sensing contacts, so contact resistance has much less effect on the result.

Four Probe Principle

Current is passed through the outer probes P1 and P4. The potential difference is measured across the inner probes P2 and P3. This gives a reliable resistivity reading for the semiconductor sample.

ρ = (V / I) × 2πs × G(W/S)

Band Gap Relation

For an intrinsic semiconductor, resistivity varies exponentially with absolute temperature. A graph of ln(ρ) against 1/T gives a line whose slope is related to the forbidden energy gap.

ln(ρ) = Eg / 2kT + C

Energy Gap

If the X-axis is plotted as 1/T × 10-3, the calculated slope must be multiplied by 103 while finding Eg.

Eg = 2k × slope × 103
Observation trend

As temperature rises, more charge carriers become available in germanium. The resistivity decreases, so ln(ρ) also decreases with increasing temperature.

Procedure

  1. Place the germanium crystal on the four probe arrangement and ensure proper contact.
  2. Connect the current source to P1 and P4, and the voltmeter to P2 and P3.
  3. Set the initial temperature and allow the sample to reach thermal equilibrium.
  4. Pass a constant current through the outer probes and record the voltage.
  5. Calculate resistivity and ln(ρ) for every recorded temperature.
  6. Plot ln(ρ) against 1/T × 10-3 and find the slope.
  7. Use Eg = 2k × slope × 103 to calculate the band gap.
