Theoretical Reference

If a light bar of breadth b and depth d is placed horizontally on two Knife-edges separated by a distance L, and a load of mass m, applied at the mid-point of the bar, produces a depression l of the bar, then Young’s modulus Y of the material of the bar is given by:

Y = (g · L³ · m) / (4 · b · d³ · l)

Where g is the acceleration due to gravity, b and d are the breadth and depth of the bar respectively.

This is the working formula of the experiment, and is valid so long as the slope of the bar at any point with respect to the unstrained position is much less than unity.

Experimental Procedure

  1. Measure the length of the given bar with a meter scale and mark its mid-point.
  2. Mount on the bar, the frame F carrying a knife-edge. Now place the bar with its least dimension vertical, on the knife-edges.
  3. Bring the knife-edge of the frame F on the central transverse mark of the bar. Focus the eye-piece on the cross-wires.
  4. Determine the vernier constant of the microscope. With zero loads on the hanger, record the position.
  5. Increasing gradually the load in steps of 0.5kg and at each observation, record the vertical scale reading.
  6. Determine the least count of the screw gauge and measure depth d of the bar at a number of places.

Required Apparatus

Screw Gauge

Slide Calipers

Travelling Microscope

Optical instrument used to measure the microscopic vertical depression (l).
