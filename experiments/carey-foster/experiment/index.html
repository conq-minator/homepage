Module 01

Theory & Background

Understanding the principles of the Carey Foster Bridge for precision resistance measurement

📌 Objective

Part 1: To determine the resistance per unit length (ρ) of the Carey Foster bridge wire.

Part 2: To find the value of an unknown low resistance (Y) using the Carey Foster bridge.

📖 Theory

The Carey Foster Bridge is a modified form of the Wheatstone Bridge, specifically designed for comparing two nearly equal resistances. It consists of a uniform wire of length 100 cm stretched along a meter scale, with four gaps. Two outer gaps contain known equal resistances P and Q (ratio arms), while the inner two gaps contain a known fractional resistance X and the unknown resistance Y.

A galvanometer is connected between the midpoint of the circuit and a sliding contact (jockey) that can be moved along the bridge wire. The balance condition is found when the galvanometer shows null deflection.

At balance, if the jockey is at a distance l from one end:

After interchanging X and Y (swapping their positions) and finding the new balance length, we can eliminate the end resistances and derive the working formulas shown to the right.

ρ = X / (l₂ − l₁)

Y = X − (l₂' − l₁') × ρ

Module 02

Procedure

Follow these steps carefully to perform the experiment

1
Draw the circuit diagram of the Carey Foster bridge. Connect the fractional resistance box X in one inner gap and short-circuit the other gap (Y = 0) using a copper strip.
2
Connect equal resistances P and Q in the outer gaps (ratio arms). These should remain constant throughout the experiment.
3
Introduce a known resistance X (say 0.5 Ω) in the resistance box. Slide the jockey along the bridge wire to find the null point (zero galvanometer deflection). Record balance length l₁.
4
Interchange (swap) the positions of X and the short-circuit wire. Again find the balance point and record the new balance length l₂.
5
Repeat steps 3–4 for different values of X (e.g., 0.5, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0, 2.5 Ω). Calculate ρ = X / (l₂ − l₁) for each. Find the mean value of ρ.
6
For Part 2, replace the short-circuit wire with the unknown resistance Y.
7
With a known X in the resistance box, find the balance length l₁'. Then swap X and Y and find the new balance length l₂'.
8
Repeat for different X values and calculate Y = X − (l₂' − l₁') × ρ for each observation. Compute the mean value of Y.
Module 02

Apparatus Required

The electrical instruments and components used in this experiment

🔌

Carey Foster Bridge

Primary Instrument

A metre bridge with four gaps. A uniform resistance wire (manganin/constantan) is stretched along a 100 cm scale. Two outer gaps hold ratio arms (P, Q) and two inner gaps hold the resistances being compared (X, Y).

Wire Length 100 cm
Wire Material Manganin / Constantan
🎛️

Fractional Resistance Box

Known Resistance Source

A precision decade resistance box providing fractional ohm values (0.1 to 5 Ω in 0.1 Ω steps). It allows setting known resistance values of X for each observation.

Range 0.1 — 5.0 Ω
Least Count 0.1 Ω
🧭

Galvanometer

Null Detector

A sensitive current-detecting instrument connected between the midpoint of the circuit (junction of P and Q) and the jockey on the bridge wire. It indicates the balance (null) condition by showing zero deflection.

Sensitivity High (µA range)
Type Moving Coil
📏

Jockey

Sliding Contact

A sliding contact that is moved gently along the bridge wire to locate the balance point. It has a sharp knife-edge for precise contact and is connected to the galvanometer via a flexible lead wire.

Contact Knife Edge
Precision ±0.1 cm
🔋

Battery / Cell

EMF Source

A Leclanché cell or a battery providing a steady EMF to drive current through the Wheatstone bridge circuit. A key switch is used for connecting and disconnecting the cell.

EMF 1.5 V (typical)
Switch Plug Key
🔗

Connecting Wires & Copper Strip

Accessories

Thick copper connecting wires for making circuit connections, and a heavy copper strip used to short-circuit one of the inner gaps (Y = 0) during Part 1 of the experiment.

Material Copper (Low R)
Strip Resistance ≈ 0 Ω

⚠️ Precautions

!
All connections should be clean and tight. Loose contacts introduce parasitic resistance.
!
The jockey should be pressed gently on the wire — do not scrape or drag it harshly, as this can damage the wire and change its cross-section.
!
The battery key should be closed only while taking readings to avoid heating of the bridge wire.
!
The balance point should ideally lie between 30 cm and 70 cm for maximum sensitivity.
!
The ratio arms P and Q should be equal and constant throughout the experiment.
