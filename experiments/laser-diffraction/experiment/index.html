Module 01

Theory & Background

Understanding the principles of laser diffraction through a transmission grating

📌 Objective

To determine the wavelength of a given LASER source by forming a diffraction pattern with a plane transmission grating.

📖 Theory

The LASER Diode module and LASER detector are mounted in place of the collimator and telescope of a spectrometer respectively. The grating is mounted on the prism base of the spectrometer. The emitted Laser beam is diffracted by the grating.

The diffraction pattern can be seen on a screen by holding it at the place of the detector. One can notice the decrease in the intensity of light as one moves away from the zeroth order towards the higher orders.

The wavelength of the LASER light is obtained from the relation:

λ = d sin θ / m
The Grating Equation — relates the wavelength to the diffraction angle
λ Wavelength of laser light
d Pitch of grating (a + b)
θ Angle of deviation
m Order of maxima
a Slit width
b Distance between slits

Module 02

Procedure

Follow these steps carefully to perform the experiment

1
Switch on the LASER source.
2
Set the spectrometer such that the source and detector should be collinear.
3
At this position, take the data of both vernier scales (Vernier I & Vernier II). These are called direct readings.
4
Now place the grating at the centre of the prism table.
5
Laser beam is now diffracted by the grating and a spectrum is formed (like Fig 1).
6
Keeping undisturbed the arrangement, take the data of both vernier scales for central order (0th order). This should coincide with direct readings.
7
Move the LASER detector towards left from the central order and set the detector at the left side 1st order. Note down both vernier scale readings in Table 1.
8
After taking 1st order data, move detector further left to the 2nd order. Note both vernier scale readings in Table 1.
9
Follow the same procedure for the left side 3rd order.
10
Now move the detector to the opposite direction and get back to the central order. Check readings match previous central order readings.
11
Move the detector towards right from the central order and repeat steps 7, 8, and 9 for right side 1st, 2nd and 3rd order.
12
Calculate the wavelength of the LASER source for 1st, 2nd and 3rd order in Table 2 using the data from Table 1.
Module 02

Apparatus Required

The optical and electronic instruments used in this experiment

🔭

Spectrometer

Primary Instrument

A precision optical instrument used to measure angles of diffraction. It features a circular scale graduated in degrees, a collimator to produce a parallel beam, and a rotatable telescope for observation.

Least Count 1/180° = 0.0056°
Verniers Dual (180° Apart)
📊

Diffraction Grating

Optical Element

A plane transmission grating consisting of an optically flat glass plate ruled with a large number of equidistant parallel lines. It diffracts incident light into monochromatic maximum points.

Grating Pitch (d) 2.000 × 10⁻⁴ cm
Lines / cm 5000 (typical)
💡

Diode LASER

Coherent Source

A semiconductor diode laser module that acts as the light source. It emits highly monochromatic, coherent, and intense light, allowing sharp diffraction fringes to form without external filters.

Wavelength (λ) 650.0 nm (Red)
Beam Type Collimated / Monochromatic
📡

Laser Detector

Sensor Unit

A photodetector mounted on the telescope arm that measures the relative intensity of diffracted beam orders. In this simulation, it captures the diffraction envelope intensity profile.

Sensor Type Silicon Photodiode
Output Relative Intensity %

⚠️ Error Analysis

From the grating equation λ = d sin θ / m, the maximum fractional error is:

(∂λ / λ)max = cot θ · ∂θ

Where ∂θ = 2 × Vernier Constant of spectrometer

Maximum % Error = (∂λ / λ)max × 100 %
